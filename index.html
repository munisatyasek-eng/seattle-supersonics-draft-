04 — METHODOLOGY

How This Draft Was Built

The rules, the math, and where every number came from.

The Model

This is a hypothetical exercise, not an NBA-sanctioned event. It's built on the same structure the league actually used the last time it expanded: the 2004 Charlotte Bobcats expansion draft. Under those real rules, each existing team could protect up to 8 players, and the expansion team could select only one player per franchise. This project applies that same one-per-team cap across a 6-team talent pool (Cavaliers, Celtics, Thunder, Rockets, Knicks, Lakers). For each team, the highest-scoring available player is identified, and the top 5 of those team-best players, ranked by combined production, become the picks.

Scope note: the real Bobcats draft selected 19 players from 29 teams; a real Seattle expansion draft would likely pull 14–15 players from a much larger league-wide pool. This project intentionally stops at a "core five", one pick per team across a 6-team sample pool, to keep the math fully visible rather than simulating a full 15-man roster from partial data. Think of it as the headline picks of a larger draft, not the complete inaugural roster.

The Formula

Each player's Combined Score is simply Points + Rebounds + Assists per game (all 2024–25 regular season averages). This is a deliberately simple, transparent stat, not an advanced metric like PER or Win Shares, chosen so the math behind every pick is visible and checkable at a glance. A more rigorous model would weight defense, efficiency, and positional need, but this keeps the exercise readable for a general audience.

Worked example: Pick #1

Payton Pritchard averaged 14.3 PPG, 3.8 RPG, and 3.5 APG in 2024–25 (NBA.com).
14.3 + 3.8 + 3.5 = 21.6
That 21.6 was the highest Combined Score of any single-team-best player in the pool, so Boston's best available player becomes Seattle's Pick #1.

Assumptions & Limitations

  • Stats are full-season 2024–25 regular-season averages. Caris LeVert was traded from Cleveland to Atlanta mid-season (Feb 6, 2025); his line reflects his combined season across both teams, not Cleveland alone.
  • Combined Score treats points, rebounds, and assists as equally weighted, which favors high-usage guards over low-stat defensive specialists (e.g. Alex Caruso, a plus defender, ranks lower under this formula despite his impact).
  • Player pool is limited to 5 bench/rotation players per team as originally selected for this project, not each team's full roster, so a "best available" pick reflects the best of that curated list, not literally every player on the team.
  • No salary cap, positional need, injury history, or trade compensation is modeled, all of which are real factors in an actual expansion draft.
